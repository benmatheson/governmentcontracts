Beltway Spending

Where does the federal government award contracts ?

The government spends billions of dollars per year on a dizzying array of items: office furniture, cloud computing, breakfast cereal, and advanced weapons systems are among destinations of taxpayer funds. We also spend deeply on expertise and services across a range of domains. Well-heeled industries have risen to meet this need and fulfill large contracts. Local companies compete with the Beltway professionals to help the U.S. government build, feed, transport, clothe and otherwise equip operations across all 50 states and territories. Where do the beltway firms execute their work? How do the contracts differ from regional and small-town operators?

A diverse American landscape of contracts

The diversity of American government contracting is as diverse as the land. In Saint Clairsville, Ohio, the only contract in the entire Ohio 6th disrtict worth more than $1 million is a Veterans Affairs Administration contract for providing healthcare for a VA clinic. IN the NY-16 district--home to the highest poverty rate of any congressional district in the nation--encompassing the northern Bronx, there are only two major contracts. Both are for pharmaceutical products: zinc pills and dalfampridine, a prescription to address multiple sclerosis patients.

Meanwhile, Virginia's 8th district boasts 3,134 million-dollar-plus contracts. The district includes Alexandria and Falls Church across its territory of Arlington County and Fairfax County.

Washington D.C. (which retains only a non-voting member of congress) had the most million-plus dollar contracts at 4206. On the opposite end of the spectrum, 34 districts hosted 10 or fewer million-dollar contracts. Of the top 10 districts by count, eight are located in Virginia or Maryland, or D.C.

Big Money things

The single most lucrative contract action in the 2016 data awards nearly $3.3 billion the Electric Boat Corporation in Groton, Connecticut, for several Virginia-class submarine hulls. Those vessels includes the "Montana", "New Jersey", "Hyman G. Rickover", and eight other submarines. The nuclear-powered submarines cost $2.68 billion per unit. About

This analysis uses USA Spending data published on the Enigma Open Data platform. It includes 2016 (the last year full data is available) federal government contracts greater than $1 million that were awarded to US-based companies and executed in the United States. Beltway include cities with a geographic center in the Washington-Arlington-Alexandria, DC-VA-MD-WV Metropolitan Statistical Area. This data represents a portion of federal expenditures. The government spends money in a number of ways including contracting, grants, credit card purchases, direct transfers, and cooperative agreements.
