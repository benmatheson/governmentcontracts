Where does the goverment spend money?

The government spends billions of dollars per year on a dizzying array of items: office furniture, cloud computing, breakfast cereal, and advanced weapons systems are among destinations of taxpayer funds. We also spend deeply on expertise and services across a range of domains. Well-heeled industries have risen to meet this need and fulfill large contracts. Local companies compete with the Beltway professionals to help the U.S. government build, feed, transport, clothe and otherwise equip operations across all 50 states and territories. Where do the beltway firms execute their work? How do the contracts differ from regional and small-town operators?
